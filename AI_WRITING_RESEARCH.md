## A 10-Page Comprehensive Guide to Identifying and Eliminating AI-isms

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** January 2025  
**Purpose:** Deep research compilation of linguistic patterns, structural markers, and stylistic cues that reveal AI-generated text, with actionable frameworks for creating genuinely human-sounding copy.

## PAGE 1: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY & TOP 10 AI-ISMS

### Why This Matters

AI-generated content is increasingly detectable through consistent linguistic patterns. These patterns create a "sameness" that readers subconsciously recognize, even if they can't articulate why. This manual identifies these patterns and provides frameworks for eliminating them.

### The Top 10 AI-isms to Eliminate Immediately

1. **Em dash overuse** — AI loves em dashes (—) for connecting thoughts. Humans use them sparingly.
2. **Period staccato** — Too many short sentences ending in periods creates a robotic rhythm.
3. **Formulaic openings** — "In today's digital landscape..." "As businesses navigate..." "It's important to note..."
4. **Triple qualifiers** — "so creative, so innovative, so undeniably effective" (AI's favorite pattern)
5. **Abstract noun abuse** — "solutions," "strategies," "methodologies," "approaches" (overused)
6. **Perfect parallelism** — Lists always perfectly structured, sentences always balanced
7. **Hedging language** — "may," "might," "could potentially," "somewhat," "in some cases"
8. **Corporate jargon** — "leverage," "synergy," "paradigm shift," "best practices," "utilize"
9. **Transition word overload** — "Furthermore," "Moreover," "Additionally," "In conclusion"
10. **Over-explanation** — Explaining obvious things, no trust in reader intelligence

### Quick Fix Framework

**Before editing:** Scan for these 10 patterns  
**During editing:** Replace with concrete, varied, direct language  
**After editing:** Read aloud—does it sound like a person talking?

## PAGE 2: LEXICAL FINGERPRINTS & VOCABULARY PATTERNS

### AI Dead Giveaways: Word-Level Patterns

#### Overused Transition Words (AI's Crutches)
- **Formal transitions:** "Furthermore," "Moreover," "Additionally," "Consequently," "Nevertheless"
- **Opening phrases:** "In today's fast-paced world," "In the realm of," "When it comes to"
- **Hedging phrases:** "It is worth noting," "It should be mentioned," "One thing to consider"
- **Conclusion markers:** "In conclusion," "To sum up," "At the end of the day"

**Human Alternative:** Direct statements, varied connectors, or no transition at all.

#### Corporate Jargon (The Buzzword Bingo)
AI defaults to corporate speak when uncertain:
- "Leverage" (use "use")
- "Synergy" (use "work together" or be specific)
- "Paradigm shift" (use "change" or describe the change)
- "Best practices" (use "what works" or be specific)
- "Utilize" (use "use")
- "Facilitate" (use "help" or "make possible")
- "Optimize" (use "improve" or be specific about what)
- "Enhance" (use "improve" or "make better")
- "Robust" (use "strong" or describe how)
- "Scalable" (use "can grow" or explain how)

#### Abstract Noun Overuse
AI loves abstract concepts because they're safe and vague:
- "Solutions" (use specific: "websites," "chatbots," "systems")
- "Strategies" (use specific: "plans," "approaches," or describe the strategy)
- "Methodologies" (use "methods" or describe the method)
- "Approaches" (use specific: "ways," "methods," or describe it)
- "Initiatives" (use "projects" or describe the project)
- "Capabilities" (use "can do" or list what it does)

#### Qualifier Patterns
AI uses qualifiers to hedge:
- **Intensifiers:** "very," "extremely," "incredibly," "particularly," "significantly"
- **Hedgers:** "may," "might," "could potentially," "somewhat," "relatively"
- **Triple patterns:** "so X, so Y, so Z" (AI's favorite structure)

**Human Pattern:** Specific claims, direct statements, varied intensity.

## PAGE 3: STRUCTURAL PATTERNS & SENTENCE ARCHITECTURE

### AI's Structural Tics

#### Perfect Three-Part Structures
AI loves lists of exactly three:
- "X, Y, and Z" appears constantly
- All paragraphs have three sentences
- All sections have three points

**Human Pattern:** Vary list lengths—some with 2, some with 4, some with 1.

#### Symmetrical Paragraphs
AI creates uniform structure:
- All paragraphs same length
- All sentences same structure
- Perfect balance everywhere

**Human Pattern:** Mix short punchy paragraphs with longer explanatory ones.

#### Formulaic Openings
AI starts paragraphs predictably:
- Every paragraph begins with a transition word
- Every section starts with context-setting
- No direct statements

**Human Pattern:** Start with the point, not the context. Vary openings.

#### Predictable Transitions
AI connects thoughts mechanically:
- "Furthermore," "Moreover," "Additionally" in every paragraph
- Perfect logical flow, no surprises
- No tangents, no asides

**Human Pattern:** Sometimes no transition needed. Sometimes use "But," "And," "So." Sometimes jump topics.

#### Over-Explanation
AI explains everything:
- Explains obvious things
- No trust in reader intelligence
- Assumes reader knows nothing

**Human Pattern:** Assume reader is smart. Skip obvious explanations.

#### No Sentence Fragments
AI writes only complete sentences:
- Everything grammatically perfect
- No intentional breaks
- No stylistic choices

**Human Pattern:** Use fragments for emphasis. "Game-changers." "Unignorable." "Actually."

#### Perfect Grammar Always
AI never breaks rules:
- No intentional errors
- No stylistic choices
- No personality in structure

**Human Pattern:** Intentional imperfection. Break rules for effect.

### Sentence Structure Analysis

#### AI Patterns:
- **Complex sentences:** Multiple clauses, perfect grammar
- **Subordinate clauses:** "While X, Y, and Z, we also..."
- **Nominalizations:** Turning verbs into nouns ("the creation of" vs "creating")
- **Prepositional phrases:** Overuse of "of," "in," "for," "with"
- **Passive voice:** "It is believed that," "It has been shown"

#### Human Patterns:
- **Simple, direct sentences:** Subject-verb-object, clear
- **Active verbs:** "We build" not "We are engaged in the building of"
- **Concrete actions:** Verbs that show doing, not being
- **Fewer prepositions:** Direct connections
- **Active voice:** "We do X" not "X is done by us"

### The Antithesis Trap

AI overuses contrast patterns:
- "We don't X, we Y" (used too often)
- "Not just X, but Y"
- Perfect parallel contrasts

**Human Pattern:** Use contrast sparingly. Vary how you make points.

## PAGE 4: PUNCTUATION & RHYTHM PATTERNS

### The Punctuation Problem

AI has specific punctuation habits that create detectable patterns:

#### Em Dash Overuse (—)
**AI Pattern:** Uses em dashes constantly to connect thoughts:
- "We don't just X—we Y"
- "Every pixel—engineered to be perfect"
- "Solutions that work—while you sleep"

**Why it's AI:** Em dashes are AI's go-to connector. Humans use them sparingly, maybe 1-2 per page.

**Human Pattern:** 
- Use commas for most connections
- Use periods to create rhythm
- Use em dashes only for dramatic pauses (rarely)
- Mix punctuation types

#### Period Staccato
**AI Pattern:** Too many short sentences ending in periods:
- "We build websites. We create experiences. We deliver results."
- Creates robotic, choppy rhythm

**Why it's AI:** AI thinks short sentences = punchy. But too many = mechanical.

**Human Pattern:**
- Mix short and long sentences
- Use commas and conjunctions to connect related thoughts
- Vary sentence length for natural rhythm
- Some fragments, some long explanations

#### Oxford Comma Overuse
**AI Pattern:** Uses Oxford comma in every list, even when unnecessary

**Human Pattern:** Use Oxford comma only when clarity requires it. Vary usage.

#### No Ellipses or Parentheses
**AI Pattern:** Rarely uses (...) or (parenthetical asides)

**Human Pattern:** Use ellipses for trailing thoughts. Use parentheses for asides.

### Rhythm & Flow Analysis

#### AI Dead Giveaways:
- **Monotonous rhythm:** Same sentence structure repeated
- **No pauses:** Everything flows without breaks
- **Perfect parallelism:** Lists always perfectly parallel
- **No emphasis:** Everything weighted equally
- **Predictable cadence:** Reads like a metronome

#### Human Patterns:
- **Varied rhythm:** Short. Then longer. Then short again.
- **Intentional pauses:** Em dashes (sparingly), ellipses, fragments
- **Emphasis through structure:** Important things get their own line
- **Asymmetrical lists:** Not everything needs to match
- **Natural cadence:** Reads like speech, not a textbook

### The Comma vs. Period Rule

**AI Default:** Periods for everything (creates staccato)

**Human Pattern:**
- Use commas to connect related thoughts
- Use periods to create emphasis or separation
- Use semicolons occasionally (humans do use them)
- Vary based on rhythm, not rules

**Example:**
- **AI:** "We build websites. We create experiences. We deliver results."
- **Human:** "We build websites that create experiences, and we deliver results that matter."

## PAGE 5: TONE & VOICE MARKERS

### AI's Tone Problems

#### Overly Enthusiastic
**AI Pattern:** Too many exclamation points or "amazing," "incredible," "revolutionary"
- "Amazing results!"
- "Incredible solutions!"
- "Revolutionary approach!"

**Human Pattern:** Enthusiasm is specific, not generic. "This cut costs by 40%" not "amazing savings!"

#### Neutral to a Fault
**AI Pattern:** No personality, no edge, no opinion
- "We provide services"
- "Our solutions help businesses"
- Generic, safe language

**Human Pattern:** Personality shows. Voice is unique. Takes stances.

#### Generic Confidence
**AI Pattern:** "We're the best" without specifics
- "World-class service"
- "Industry-leading solutions"
- "Unparalleled expertise"

**Human Pattern:** Confidence backed by specifics. "We've built 200+ websites" not "industry-leading."

#### Apologetic Hedging
**AI Pattern:** "We believe," "We think," "In our opinion"
- "We believe our solutions are effective"
- "We think you'll be satisfied"
- "In our opinion, this is the best approach"

**Human Pattern:** Direct statements. "Our solutions work" not "We believe our solutions may work."

#### Corporate Speak
**AI Pattern:** Meaningless phrases
- "We're committed to excellence"
- "Dedicated to your success"
- "Passionate about innovation"

**Human Pattern:** Show, don't tell. Specific actions, not abstract commitments.

#### No Vulnerability
**AI Pattern:** Never admits limitations or trade-offs
- Everything is perfect
- No downsides mentioned
- No honesty about challenges

**Human Pattern:** Admits reality. "These aren't just tools" (acknowledges they could be seen as tools).

#### Perfect Politeness
**AI Pattern:** Never challenges or provokes
- Always agreeable
- Never controversial
- No edge

**Human Pattern:** Can challenge. "Your competition won't see this coming" (provocative, confident).

### Emotional Intelligence Markers

#### AI Dead Giveaways:
- **No emotional range:** Stays in one emotional register
- **Generic emotions:** "excited," "thrilled," "amazing"
- **No subtext:** Everything is surface level
- **Perfect emotional logic:** Emotions always make sense

#### Human Patterns:
- **Emotional variety:** Confidence, challenge, excitement, directness
- **Specific emotions:** "undeniable," "unignorable" (more specific than "great")
- **Subtext:** What's not said matters
- **Emotional contradictions:** Can be confident and challenging simultaneously

### Voice Authenticity Checklist

- [ ] Does this sound like a real person talking?
- [ ] Could only this company/person write this?
- [ ] Is there personality, not just information?
- [ ] Are there opinions, not just facts?
- [ ] Is there edge, not just politeness?

## PAGE 6: CONTENT PATTERNS & SPECIFICITY

### AI's Content Problems

#### Generic Benefits
**AI Pattern:** Vague, universal claims
- "Save time"
- "Increase efficiency"
- "Boost productivity"
- "Improve results"

**Human Pattern:** Specific outcomes with proof
- "Turn missed opportunities into revenue"
- "Cut response time from 24 hours to 2 minutes"
- "Convert 30% more leads"

#### No Specific Examples
**AI Pattern:** Vague claims without proof
- "Our solutions are effective"
- "We deliver great results"
- "Clients love our work"

**Human Pattern:** Concrete examples
- "Voice agents so natural, customers won't know they're AI"
- "Every pixel engineered to be unignorable"
- "200+ websites built, zero downtime"

#### Over-Promising
**AI Pattern:** "Revolutionary," "game-changing" without substance
- "Revolutionary approach"
- "Game-changing solutions"
- "Transform your business"

**Human Pattern:** Specific claims, not hyperbole
- "We build what others say can't be done"
- "Solutions your competition hasn't imagined"
- Concrete outcomes, not abstract promises

#### Feature Lists
**AI Pattern:** Lists what it does, not why it matters
- "Our platform includes X, Y, Z features"
- "We offer comprehensive solutions"
- Feature-focused, not benefit-focused

**Human Pattern:** Benefit-focused
- What happens for the reader
- Outcomes, not features
- "Your site runs while you sleep" not "automated maintenance"

#### No Stories
**AI Pattern:** Pure information, no narrative
- Facts and features only
- No metaphors, no analogies
- No human connection

**Human Pattern:** Narrative elements
- Stories, metaphors, analogies
- "We don't build websites. We build conversion machines."
- Human connection through narrative

#### Perfect Logic
**AI Pattern:** Everything flows perfectly, no tangents
- Linear thinking only
- No contradictions
- No surprises

**Human Pattern:** Contradictions for emphasis
- "We don't build websites. We create digital experiences."
- Can challenge its own statements
- Tangents that add personality

### Specificity Framework

#### Abstract → Concrete
- "Solutions" → "websites," "chatbots," "systems"
- "Strategies" → specific plans or approaches
- "Services" → list what you actually do

#### Vague → Specific
- "Improve" → "increase by 30%"
- "Help" → "turn X into Y"
- "Better" → "faster," "cheaper," "more effective" (with numbers)

#### Generic → Named
- "Businesses" → "e-commerce stores," "agencies," "SaaS companies"
- "Clients" → "past customers," "repeat buyers"
- "Results" → specific outcomes

### The Proof Principle

**AI Says:** "We deliver great results"  
**Human Says:** "We've built 200+ websites with zero downtime"

**AI Says:** "Our solutions are effective"  
**Human Says:** "Our chatbots handle 80% of customer inquiries without human intervention"

**Rule:** Every claim needs proof or specificity.

### Before/After Examples

#### Example 1: Hero Section

**BEFORE (AI):**
```
Creative Solutions for Modern Businesses
In today's digital landscape, businesses need innovative solutions that leverage cutting-edge technology to facilitate growth and optimize performance. Our comprehensive approach provides valuable insights and delivers exceptional results.
```

**AFTER (Human):**
```
Creative Solutions; Because Most Problems Can't Be Solved Inside the Box
Unignorable. Undeniable. Alien Brains. We build things your competition hasn't imagined yet.
```

**Changes Made:**
- Removed formulaic opening
- Removed corporate jargon ("leverage," "facilitate," "optimize")
- Removed abstract nouns ("solutions," "approach")
- Added fragments for emphasis
- Made claims specific and direct
- Added personality ("Alien Brains")

#### Example 2: Service Description

**BEFORE (AI):**
```
Website Development
We don't just build websites. We create digital experiences that your competition can't replicate. Every pixel, every interaction, every conversion path is engineered to be unignorable through our comprehensive methodology.
```

**AFTER (Human):**
```
Website Development
We don't build websites. We build conversion machines. Every pixel, every click, every path to purchase engineered to make your competition look like they're still using dial-up.
```

**Changes Made:**
- "digital experiences" → "conversion machines" (concrete)
- Removed "comprehensive methodology" (corporate jargon)
- "can't replicate" → "look like they're still using dial-up" (specific, visual)
- Removed hedging and over-explanation
- More direct, less corporate

#### Example 3: About Section

**BEFORE (AI):**
```
About Playbook MPR
Playbook MPR creates AI-powered solutions so creative, so innovative, so undeniably effective that your competition won't see them coming. We build automated systems that others wouldn't think possible—solutions that strengthen customer relationships, prevent revenue loss, and transform how your business operates. These aren't just tools. They're game-changers.
```

**AFTER (Human):**
```
Alien Brains. Unignorable Ideas.

We don't think inside the box. We don't even acknowledge the box exists.

Playbook MPR builds AI that actually works. Not the kind that promises the moon and delivers a memo. The kind that turns missed calls into customers, past buyers into repeat revenue, and your out-of-hours into your competitive edge. We build stuff others say can't be done. Then we ship it. These aren't tools. They're unfair advantages.
```

**Changes Made:**
- Removed triple qualifier ("so creative, so innovative, so undeniably")
- "solutions" → "AI" (concrete)
- Removed em dash, varied punctuation
- Broke up long sentence
- "transform how your business operates" → specific outcomes
- "game-changers" → "unfair advantages" (more specific)
- Added contractions and conversational tone
- Varied sentence length significantly

- **URL:** pangram.com/blog/walking-through-ai-phrases

### Manual Detection Methods

#### The Read-Aloud Test
**Process:**
1. Read your copy aloud
2. Does it sound natural?
3. Would you say this in conversation?
4. Does it flow like speech?

**Red Flags:**
- Sounds robotic
- Too formal for context
- Unnatural rhythm
- Wouldn't say this out loud

#### The "Would I Write This?" Test
**Process:**
1. Read each sentence
2. Ask: "Would I naturally write this?"
3. If no, rewrite it

**Red Flags:**
- Too perfect
- Too generic
- No personality
- Sounds like a template

#### The Pattern Recognition Test
**Process:**
1. Scan for repeated patterns
2. Look for perfect structures
3. Check for formulaic openings
4. Identify overused words

**Red Flags:**
- Same sentence structure repeated
- Perfect lists of three
- Every paragraph starts the same way
- Same transition words throughout

### Validation Checklist

After editing, validate:
- [ ] Ran through GPTZero (if available)
- [ ] Scanned for common AI phrases
- [ ] Read aloud test passed
- [ ] "Would I write this?" test passed
- [ ] No repeated patterns
- [ ] Varied structure throughout
- [ ] Personality and voice present
- [ ] Specific claims, not vague
- [ ] Natural rhythm

### What to Trust (and What Not To)

**Trust:**
- Your read-aloud test
- Pattern recognition (repeated structures)
- Your gut (if it sounds AI, it probably is)
- Specific detection tools as baseline

**Don't Trust:**
- Tools as absolute truth (they're not perfect)
- Perfect grammar scores (humans break rules)
- Generic "writing quality" scores
- AI's own assessment of its writing

### The Human Editor's Advantage

**Humans can detect:**
- Subtle rhythm issues
- Voice authenticity
- Emotional subtext
- Cultural context
- Nuance and personality

**Tools can detect:**
- Statistical patterns
- Common phrases
- Structural similarities
- Word frequency

**Best Approach:** Use tools for initial scan, human judgment for final validation.
