# AI Writing Detection: Dead Giveaways & Human Writing Patterns
## Harvard-Level Research Analysis

### Executive Summary
This document identifies linguistic patterns, structural markers, and stylistic cues that reveal AI-generated text. Understanding these patterns enables creation of genuinely human-sounding copy that avoids detection.

---

## 1. LEXICAL & VOCABULARY PATTERNS

### AI Dead Giveaways:
- **Overuse of transition words**: "Furthermore," "Moreover," "Additionally," "In conclusion"
- **Formal language in casual contexts**: "Utilize" instead of "use," "Facilitate" instead of "help"
- **Repetitive qualifiers**: "very," "extremely," "incredibly," "particularly"
- **Corporate jargon**: "Leverage," "synergy," "paradigm shift," "best practices"
- **Abstract nouns**: "solutions," "strategies," "methodologies," "approaches"
- **Passive voice overuse**: "It is believed that," "It has been shown"
- **Hedging language**: "may," "might," "could potentially," "somewhat"

### Human Writing Patterns:
- **Specific, concrete nouns**: "website" not "digital solution," "chatbot" not "conversational interface"
- **Active voice**: "We build" not "It is built"
- **Contractions**: "don't," "won't," "can't" (when appropriate)
- **Direct statements**: "We do X" not "We may potentially facilitate X"
- **Varied sentence length**: Mix of short punches and longer explanations
- **Idioms and colloquialisms**: "game-changer," "no-brainer," "think outside the box"

---

## 2. STRUCTURAL PATTERNS

### AI Dead Giveaways:
- **Perfect three-part structures**: Lists of exactly 3 items repeatedly
- **Symmetrical paragraphs**: All paragraphs same length
- **Formulaic openings**: "In today's digital landscape..." "As businesses navigate..."
- **Predictable transitions**: Every paragraph starts with a transition word
- **Over-explanation**: Explaining obvious things
- **No sentence fragments**: Everything is a complete sentence
- **Perfect grammar**: No intentional breaks, no stylistic choices

### Human Writing Patterns:
- **Asymmetrical structure**: Some lists with 2 items, some with 4, some with 1
- **Varied paragraph length**: Short punchy ones mixed with longer explanatory ones
- **Direct openings**: Start with the point, not the context
- **Intentional fragments**: "Game-changers." "Unignorable."
- **Imperfect but intentional**: Occasional stylistic choices that break "rules"
- **Conversational flow**: Reads like someone talking, not a manual

---

## 3. TONE & VOICE MARKERS

### AI Dead Giveaways:
- **Overly enthusiastic**: Too many exclamation points or "amazing," "incredible"
- **Neutral to a fault**: No personality, no edge, no opinion
- **Generic confidence**: "We're the best" without specifics
- **Apologetic hedging**: "We believe," "We think," "In our opinion"
- **Corporate speak**: "We're committed to excellence" (meaningless)
- **No vulnerability**: Never admits limitations or trade-offs
- **Perfect politeness**: Never challenges or provokes

### Human Writing Patterns:
- **Confident without bragging**: Specific claims backed by evidence
- **Personality**: Voice that could only come from this person/company
- **Direct challenges**: "Your competition won't see this coming"
- **Admits reality**: "These aren't just tools" (acknowledges they could be seen as tools)
- **Opinionated**: Takes a stance, doesn't hedge
- **Conversational confidence**: "We don't even acknowledge the box exists"

---

## 4. CONTENT PATTERNS

### AI Dead Giveaways:
- **Generic benefits**: "Save time," "Increase efficiency," "Boost productivity"
- **No specific examples**: Vague claims without proof
- **Over-promising**: "Revolutionary," "game-changing" without substance
- **Feature lists**: Lists what it does, not why it matters
- **No stories**: Pure information, no narrative
- **Perfect logic**: Everything flows perfectly, no tangents
- **No contradictions**: Never challenges its own statements

### Human Writing Patterns:
- **Specific outcomes**: "Turn missed opportunities into revenue" not "improve sales"
- **Concrete examples**: "Voice agents so natural, customers won't know they're AI"
- **Proof in claims**: "Every pixel engineered to be unignorable"
- **Benefit-focused**: What happens for the reader, not what the tool does
- **Narrative elements**: Stories, metaphors, analogies
- **Contradictions for emphasis**: "We don't build websites. We create digital experiences."

---

## 5. RHYTHM & FLOW

### AI Dead Giveaways:
- **Monotonous rhythm**: Same sentence structure repeated
- **No pauses**: Everything flows without breaks
- **Perfect parallelism**: Lists always perfectly parallel
- **No emphasis**: Everything weighted equally
- **Predictable cadence**: Reads like a metronome

### Human Writing Patterns:
- **Varied rhythm**: Short. Then longer. Then short again.
- **Intentional pauses**: Em dashes, ellipses, fragments
- **Emphasis through structure**: Important things get their own line
- **Asymmetrical lists**: Not everything needs to match
- **Natural cadence**: Reads like speech, not a textbook

---

## 6. SPECIFIC PHRASES TO AVOID

### AI Red Flags:
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "It's important to note that..."
- "With that said..."
- "In order to..."
- "As a result of..."
- "It is worth mentioning..."
- "One thing to consider..."
- "It should be noted that..."
- "When it comes to..."

### Human Alternatives:
- Direct statements
- "Here's the thing..."
- "Look..."
- "Here's what happens..."
- "The reality is..."
- "Here's the deal..."

---

## 7. SENTENCE STRUCTURE ANALYSIS

### AI Patterns:
- **Complex sentences**: Multiple clauses, perfect grammar
- **Subordinate clauses**: "While X, Y, and Z, we also..."
- **Nominalizations**: Turning verbs into nouns ("the creation of" vs "creating")
- **Prepositional phrases**: Overuse of "of," "in," "for," "with"

### Human Patterns:
- **Simple, direct sentences**: Subject-verb-object, clear
- **Active verbs**: "We build" not "We are engaged in the building of"
- **Concrete actions**: Verbs that show doing, not being
- **Fewer prepositions**: Direct connections

---

## 8. EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE MARKERS

### AI Dead Giveaways:
- **No emotional range**: Stays in one emotional register
- **Generic emotions**: "excited," "thrilled," "amazing"
- **No subtext**: Everything is surface level
- **Perfect emotional logic**: Emotions always make sense

### Human Patterns:
- **Emotional variety**: Confidence, challenge, excitement, directness
- **Specific emotions**: "undeniable," "unignorable" (more specific than "great")
- **Subtext**: What's not said matters
- **Emotional contradictions**: Can be confident and challenging simultaneously

---

## 9. SPECIFICITY & CONCRETENESS

### AI Patterns:
- **Abstract concepts**: "solutions," "strategies," "approaches"
- **Vague metrics**: "improve," "enhance," "optimize"
- **Generic examples**: "businesses," "companies," "organizations"

### Human Patterns:
- **Concrete nouns**: "websites," "chatbots," "newsletters"
- **Specific metrics**: "From £500," "£49/month"
- **Concrete examples**: "past customers," "out-of-hours calls"

---

## 10. CONVERSATIONAL MARKERS

### AI Avoids:
- Contractions (in formal contexts)
- Slang
- Regional expressions
- Personal pronouns in business contexts
- Direct address ("you")

### Human Uses:
- Contractions when natural
- Industry-appropriate slang ("game-changer")
- Personal voice
- Direct "you" address
- Conversational flow

---

## APPLICATION FRAMEWORK

### When Rewriting AI-Generated Copy:

1. **Replace abstract with concrete**: "solutions" → specific things you build
2. **Break perfect structures**: Vary list lengths, paragraph sizes
3. **Add personality**: Voice that's uniquely yours
4. **Use fragments**: Intentional breaks for emphasis
5. **Vary sentence length**: Mix short punches with longer explanations
6. **Remove hedging**: "may" → "will" or "do"
7. **Add contractions**: Where natural
8. **Use active voice**: "We build" not "It is built"
9. **Be specific**: Concrete examples, not vague benefits
10. **Challenge norms**: "We don't X, we Y"

---

## CONCLUSION

Human writing is imperfect, varied, and personal. AI writing is perfect, uniform, and generic. To sound human, embrace imperfection, vary structure, and inject personality. The goal isn't to write perfectly—it's to write like a real person with something to say.
